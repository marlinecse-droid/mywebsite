Why Little Wonders

Not your average kindergarten.

Most early childhood centres run on routines and repetition. We believe children thrive when they're genuinely seen, heard, and trusted.

🌿

Nature-led learning spaces

Our campus features a living garden, mud kitchen, and open-air classroom. Children learn science, math, and empathy outdoors — not just inside four walls.

✏️

Emergent curriculum

Lessons follow children's own questions and discoveries. A child's wonder about a snail becomes a week of science, storytelling, and art.

❤️

Social-emotional focus

Every educator is trained in trauma-informed care and positive discipline. We teach children how to name feelings, not suppress them.

🌍

Multilingual environment

English, Hindi, and Telugu are woven naturally into daily life. Children absorb language from context, conversation, and stories — not drills.

🎨

Dedicated arts & music studio

A fully equipped studio with clay, canvas, instruments, and a recording nook. Creativity is treated as a core subject, not a Friday afternoon filler.

📊

Transparent progress portfolios

Parents receive living digital portfolios — photos, observations, and developmental notes — updated weekly, not just at end-of-term report cards.

Our Story

Founded by teachers, for children.

Little Wonders Academy was started in 2009 by a group of early childhood educators who were tired of cookie-cutter classrooms. We built the school we always wished existed: warm, rigorous, and radically child-centred. Every decision — from furniture height to nap schedules — is made with the child's point of view first.

Our Approach

Four pillars. One childhood.

Everything we do is grounded in decades of developmental research and the simple truth that children learn best when they feel safe, loved, and free to explore.

01

Play is learning

Unstructured and guided play is the primary mode of learning for children under six. We protect this time fiercely from academic pressure.

02

Relationships first

A secure attachment to a trusted adult is the foundation all learning rests on. Our low ratios ensure no child is invisible.

03

The child as expert

Children are competent, curious, and capable. We observe before we intervene, and follow their lead before imposing ours.

04

Community matters

Families are co-educators. We involve parents not as observers but as active partners in their child's learning story.
